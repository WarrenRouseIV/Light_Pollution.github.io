Light pollution is excessive, misdirected, or obtrusive artificial (usually outdoor) light. Components of light pollution include glare – excessive brightness that causes visual discomfort, skyglow – brightening of the night sky over inhabited areas, light trespass – light falling where it is not intended or needed, and clutter – bright confusing and excessive groupings of light sources. A common example of spill light is light from a streetlight coming through a window and illuminating a bedroom, light from outdoor wall lights that direct light up towards the sky rather than towards the ground, or light from a neighbor's floodlight or security light shining over the fence and illuminating your property. These components and light pollution as a whole pose a threat to the health of humans and wildlife, contribution to climate change, an absurd amount of energy consumption, safety for humans and wildlife, and our ability to see stars at night.
